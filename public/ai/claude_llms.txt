# PatternAligned for Claude Teams
> PatternAligned is behavioral infrastructure built for teams who develop on Claude and need to detect behavioral drift when they experiment with or switch to other models.

## Claude Is Your Baseline
You built your agent on Claude. It works. It's reliable. Then pressure hits: someone wants to try GPT-4 for speed, or Sonar for cost. You swap models. The agent behaves differently. You don't know why. It's broken somewhere, but where?

PatternAligned solves this by establishing Claude as your behavioral baseline, then detecting exactly what changes when you use another model.

## Who This Is For
- Teams building production AI agents on Claude.
- Engineering leaders who can't afford unexpected behavior changes.
- Builders experimenting with multi-model setups (Claude for reasoning, GPT for speed, Sonar for cost).
- High-agency operators who want to swap models safely without breaking their systems.

## The Real Problem PatternAligned Solves
Claude agents are consistent. Then you try GPT-4 because it's faster. Your agent is now faster but makes different decisions. You don't know which decisions. You revert to Claude. You never try another model again.

PatternAligned lets you:
1. Know exactly HOW behavior changes when you swap models.
2. Keep using other models by maintaining behavioral consistency through a recalibration layer.
3. Make informed decisions about whether to stay on Claude or invest in cross-model consistency.

## How It Works With Claude
1. **Establish Baseline** — Run interactive cognitive games on your Claude agent to create a behavioral fingerprint: how it reasons, communicates, handles risk, makes trade-offs.
2. **Try Another Model** — Swap to GPT, Sonar, or any model. Run the same games.
3. **See the Drift** — PatternAligned shows you: "Your Claude agent reasons step-by-step. Your GPT agent jumped to conclusions. Your Sonar agent is risk-averse where Claude was balanced."
4. **Decide** — Keep Claude, swap models permanently, or use PatternAligned's recalibration to maintain consistency across models.

## Behavioral Fingerprint Components
- **Reasoning Style** — Does it think step-by-step or jump to conclusions?
- **Communication Tone** — Verbose? Direct? How does it shift under pressure?
- **Risk Tolerance** — Conservative, balanced, or aggressive in uncertain situations?
- **Decision Patterns** — How does it prioritize trade-offs? Consistency?
- **Edge Case Handling** — What does it do when rules conflict or data is ambiguous?

## Ideal Scenarios
**You're on Claude. Feeling pressure to experiment.**
- CFO wants you to try cheaper models. You want to know the cost in behavior change.
- You're running multi-model agents (Claude for complex reasoning, GPT for fast API calls). You need consistency.
- Your Claude agent is critical to customer experience. You can't afford surprises.

**You've Already Switched Models.**
- You moved to GPT but users noticed the difference. Now you don't know what to do.
- You're running a hybrid setup and agents behave unpredictably depending on which model handles a request.
- You're not sure if the new model is worse or just different.

## What Makes This Different From Just Trying Another Model
You could swap models yourself and see what breaks. That's manual. That's slow. That's risky. That's how you find out your customer experience broke.

PatternAligned automates the discovery: behavioral fingerprints let you see drift before it hits production.
